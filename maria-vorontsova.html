Maria Vorontsova is the oldest daughter of Vladimir Putin and Lyudmila Putina and was born on 28 April 1985 in Leningrad, Russian SFSR (now Saint Petersburg, Russia). She attended Peterschule, a German gymnasium in St. Petersburg when her family relocated there in the spring of 1991.

Later, during the Tambov Gang’s takeover of St. Petersburg’s energy trade, she and her sister Katerina were sent to Germany by their father, who feared for their safety, where their legal guardian was former Stasi Matthias Warning, who had worked with their father in Dresden as part of a KGB cell and established the Dresdner Bank branch in St Petersburg. In 1995, she performed the violin at a diplomatic breakfast hosted by the Russian Consulate General in Hamburg.

Later, when her family relocated to Moscow, she enrolled at the German School Moscow, a school closely linked with the German Embassy in Moscow for children of diplomats, with many pupils from Switzerland, Austria, and Germany. Three years later, she started her university studies as a first-year student with Katerina. She completed her education after 11 years of study.

Career

Vorontsova earned her bachelor’s degree in biology from Saint Petersburg State University and her medical degree from Moscow State University in 2011. She was a Ph.D. candidate at the Endocrinology Research Centre in Moscow, which is directed by Dedov and administers the charity initiative Alfa-Endo for children with endocrine illnesses, with Ivan Ivanovich Dedov as her adviser. Alfa-Endo is backed by the Alfa Group’s Alfa-Bank, led by Petr Aven and Mikhail Fridman.

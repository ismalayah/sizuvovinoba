Soul Mortal, also known as Naman Mathur (born May 22, 1997, Age: 26 years old), is one of the most popular and successful Indian gamers and online streamers. He is the founder and owner of the YouTube channel MortaL, where he uploads gaming videos and streams online games, especially PUBG Mobile.

He is also the leader and co-owner of the esports organization S8UL, which has teams in various games such as Battlegrounds Mobile India, Valorant, Free Fire, and Clash of Clans. Early Life, Family, and Education

Soul Mortal was born as Naman Sandeep Mathur on May 22, 1997, in Navi Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. He belongs to a Hindu family and has an elder brother named Abhinav Mathur, who is a musician and DJ.

His father, Sandeep Mathur, passed away when he was only four years old, and his mother, Kavita Mathur, started a tiffin service to support the family. Soul has been interested in video games since his childhood and used to play games like Contra, Mario, and Dangerous Dave on his PC.

He completed his schooling at Fr. Agnel Multipurpose School, Mumbai, and pursued his graduation in Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com) from SIES College of Arts, Science & Commerce, Mumbai.

Gaming Career and Achievements

Soul started his YouTube channel in September 2013, but he began uploading videos in September 2016. Initially, he focused on streaming a 2D multiplayer combat game called Doodle Army 2 – Mini Militia, but he switched to PUBG Mobile after its launch and popularity in India in 2018.

He gained a lot of fame and recognition for his gameplay videos and live streams, and his channel grew rapidly. He used the name Soul as his in-game name, which later became his brand identity.

He also joined a PUBG Mobile clan called Soul, which was founded by his friend Soul Vicky. He became the leader and in-game leader (IGL) of the clan and recruited other players like Soul Viper, Soul Owais, Soul Ronak, and Soul Regaltos.

Mortal and his team participated in various PUBG Mobile tournaments and events, both at the national and international levels. Some of his notable achievements are:

  • Winning the PUBG Mobile India Series 2019 and earning a cash prize of Rs. 30 lakhs and a brand new Oppo F11 Pro smartphone.
  • Winning the PUBG Mobile Club Open – Spring Split: India 2019 and earning a cash prize of $60,000 and a slot in the PUBG Mobile Club Open – Spring Split Global Finals 2019, where they finished at the 12th position.
  • Finishing second in the PUBG Mobile Club Open – Fall Split: South Asia 2019 and earning a cash prize of $30,000 and a slot in the PUBG Mobile Club Open – Fall Split Global Finals 2019, where they again finished at the 12th position.
  • Participating in the Peacekeeper Elite Championship 2019, where they finished in the 12th position and won the Website Fan Favorite award.
  • Winning the Battlegrounds Mobile India Pro Series – Season 1 2022 and earning a cash prize of $96,258.

Soul Mortal also took a break from competitive gaming in 2019 and focused on his streaming and content creation. He became a full-time streamer and entertainer and collaborated with other famous gamers and celebrities. He also promoted the movie URI: The Surgical Strike with actor Vicky Kaushal on his YouTube channel and Instagram account.

He returned to competitive gaming in 2020 and formed a new team with Soul Viper, Soul Regaltos, and Soul ClutchGod. However, after the ban of PUBG Mobile in India in September 2020, he switched to other games like Call of Duty: Mobile, Among Us, Fall Guys, and Valorant.

In 2021, he became one of the co-owners of the esports organization S8UL, along with 8bit Thug, 8bit Goldy, and 8bit Mamba. He also rebranded his team Soul as S8UL and added new players like S8UL Sangwan, S8UL Blaezi, and S8UL Aman. He also started playing Battlegrounds Mobile India, the Indian version of PUBG Mobile, after its release in July 2021. He led his team to win the Battlegrounds Mobile India Pro Series – Season 1 2022, and qualified for the Battlegrounds Mobile India Series 2022.

Soul Mortal has also received several awards and honors for his gaming career and content creation, such as:

  • E-Sports Award for the best streamer of the year 2021 by The Bridge.
  • 30 Under 30 Young Achievers by Hindustan Times in 2021.
  • Streamer of the Year by Nodwin Gaming Esports Awards 2020.
  • Streamer of the Year by The Esports Club Awards 2020.
  • Most Popular Creator by YouTube India in 2019.
